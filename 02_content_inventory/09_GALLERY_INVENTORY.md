## Purpose

This file tracks candidate work, images, visual assets, and gallery material for the Work / Gallery section of mikamutzki.com.

It is a draft evidence source, not a final gallery curation, not final website copy, and not a final visual direction document.

The goal is to prevent unreviewed visuals, old exports, screenshots, experiments, or AI-generated material from becoming portfolio truth by accident.

The Gallery should support Phase 1 as a curated visual proof layer. It should show Mika's visual range and selected work without forcing every item into a full case-study narrative.

## Gallery role in Phase 1

The Work / Gallery section is the curated visual proof layer for Phase 1.

It should:

- give quick access to selected work
- make Floom and CUPRA easy to find
- show visual range and senior Art Direction judgment
- separate full case studies from broader visual work
- avoid fake project narratives
- avoid invented clients, outcomes, metrics, responsibilities, or permissions
- allow some items to be shown as visual proof without full case-study depth

It does not need to be exhaustive for Phase 1.

## Minimum viable Phase 1 Gallery

For Phase 1, the Gallery can be lean.

Recommended minimum:

1. Floom case card
2. CUPRA case card
3. A small curated grid of selected visual work, only where assets are confirmed and safe
4. Clear labels where work is conceptual, reconstructed, remastered, or withheld
5. No invented case narratives for gallery-only pieces

The Gallery should not delay launch if Floom and CUPRA carry the main proof layer and the broader visual selection is still limited.

---

## Gallery content rules

### Allowed

- confirmed real work
- confirmed case-entry cards for Floom and CUPRA
- visual work with clear source and permission status
- conceptual or lab work when clearly labelled
- reconstructed or remastered visuals when clearly labelled
- short project captions where claims are confirmed

### Avoid

- fake clients
- fake campaign outcomes
- fake metrics
- unverified responsibilities
- unapproved brand logos
- original client assets without permission review
- AI-generated visuals presented as client work
- old screenshots treated as current proof without review
- generic portfolio filler
- gallery items that weaken the senior first impression

---

## Caption rules

Every gallery item should eventually have:

- title
- year or timeframe, if confirmed
- context: client / agency / self-initiated / lab / education / archive
- Mika's role, only where confirmed
- asset label: Original / Remastered / Reconstructed / Conceptual / Anonymised / Withheld / Needs permission
- one precise line explaining why it is shown
- claim-risk note, if relevant

Do not write long case narratives for Gallery-only items unless a dedicated case source exists.

---

## Visual quality criteria

A Gallery item should earn its place by supporting at least one of the following:

- UX/UI craft
- Art Direction judgment
- typography and layout control
- campaign or visual-system thinking
- AI-assisted creative workflow maturity
- image curation and editing judgment
- digital design experience
- senior-level visual taste

Remove or postpone anything that feels generic, decorative, low-resolution, overprocessed, template-based, or unclear.

---

## Permission and claim risk

Before public use, clarify for each item:

- Can the client or brand be named?
- Can logos be shown?
- Can original assets be shown?
- Does the asset contain proprietary interface, campaign, customer, or internal material?
- Is the work original, remastered, reconstructed, anonymised, conceptual, or withheld?
- Does the caption imply a role or outcome that is not confirmed?
- Does the item need a disclaimer?

No permission found does not mean permission exists.
