## Purpose

This file records the currently available and reviewable evidence for the CUPRA case.

It is a draft evidence source, not final website copy and not a final case-study structure.

The goal is to preserve confirmed facts, working evidence, claim boundaries, asset risks, and remaining open questions so the CUPRA case can later be written without inventing roles, outcomes, app functionality, campaign results, tools, permissions, or responsibilities.

CUPRA is planned as the second key Phase 1 portfolio case after Floom, likely under `/work/cupra`. The case should support Mika's positioning in Art Direction, AI-assisted visual production, prompting, visual curation, and responsible claim control.

## Project summary

CUPRA is planned as an Art Direction and AI-assisted visual production case.

The safest factual core is:

> Mika worked as Art Director at Shanghai Berlin and contributed to AI-based visual motifs used by CUPRA for an Easter activation in the Munich showroom in 2023. His contribution involved prompting and output preparation / Ausspielung.

The case should be framed as a real agency-side contribution to AI-based visual production, not as complete campaign ownership, full app ownership, live AI technology development, or a measurable business-outcome case.

The experience / app must not be described as live AI generation. Prior working context states that the experience simulated a generative AI interaction through a curated library of pre-generated AI images. This is an internal claim-control guardrail. Before using the exact mechanics publicly, the interface and asset proof should be reviewed or reconfirmed.
