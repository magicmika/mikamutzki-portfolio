Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This file defines what "sendable" means for Phase 1.

Phase 1 is not the final portfolio. Phase 1 is a Bewerbung-ready intermediate release: credible, usable, senior-worthy, and transparent about what will be improved after launch.

This file is the single, authoritative source for the Phase 1 Definition of Done. Any earlier preliminary Phase 1 DoD notes are no longer maintained in `01_PROJECT_CHARTER.md`.

This file exists to prevent perfectionism, scope creep, and unclear launch decisions.

---

## Definition of sendable

Phase 1 is sendable when Mika can use the portfolio link in applications without needing to explain why essential content, navigation, cases, contact routes, or legal pages are missing.

The release may still have known follow-up tasks, but those tasks must not block the first credible, application-ready version.

---

## Confirmed Phase 1 pages

Phase 1 must include:

- Home
- Work / Gallery
- Floom case
- CUPRA case
- Contact
- Impressum
- Datenschutz

These pages are required for the first sendable release.

---

## Minimum acceptance criteria

### 1. Home

Home is complete enough when it:

- introduces Mika clearly
- makes the portfolio purpose understandable within seconds
- points users toward real work
- does not rely on generic portfolio claims
- does not over-explain the entire future portfolio

Home does not need to contain every future section.

### 2. Work / Gallery

Work / Gallery is complete enough when it:

- gives access to selected work
- clearly separates case-study content from broader visual work
- makes Floom and CUPRA easy to find
- does not invent project descriptions, outcomes, KPIs, or responsibilities
- does not require a complete long-form explanation for every gallery item

Work / Gallery may be expanded after launch.

### 3. Floom case

Floom is complete enough when it:

- has a dedicated case page
- communicates what the project is
- explains Mika's role only where confirmed
- shows relevant UX/UI thinking and visible work
- includes enough structure to be understood by a senior design reviewer
- avoids unnecessary process theatre

Floom does not need to be a perfect final case study at launch.

### 4. CUPRA case

CUPRA is complete enough when it:

- has a dedicated case page
- communicates the nature of the work without inflated claims
- shows relevant visual or art-direction evidence where available
- clearly avoids invented KPIs, campaign outcomes, responsibilities, or permissions
- supports Mika's positioning as Art Direction / UX/UI / AI-assisted creative professional

CUPRA may be shorter than Floom if available evidence is more limited.

### 5. Contact

Contact is complete enough when it:

- provides a usable way to contact Mika
- is easy to find
- does not create unnecessary friction
- does not depend on unfinished future features

### 6. Legal pages

Impressum and Datenschutz are complete enough when they:

- exist as accessible pages
- are reachable from the site
- are functional and readable
- are not treated as visual experiments

Legal content must not be invented.

---

## Global release criteria

Phase 1 can be considered sendable only when:

- a live URL exists
- all confirmed Phase 1 pages exist
- navigation to all required pages works
- the portfolio can be reviewed on desktop and mobile without major blocking issues
- content does not contain invented claims
- links and primary interactions work
- no page feels obviously broken, empty, or placeholder-based
- unfinished future sections are not presented as already complete
- the site gives a credible senior first impression
- no known critical design-system violation remains
- known post-launch improvements are listed separately

---

## Not required for Phase 1

The following items are not required before the first sendable release:

- About page
- AI Practice page
- Leadership page
- Lab page
- full design-system documentation
- complete case library
- complex motion system
- perfect visual polish
- complete bilingual implementation
- full long-form process documentation for every project
- final future-state information architecture

These items may be planned after launch.

---

## Post-launch improvement list

Known follow-up work should be tracked after launch, for example:

- refine visual polish
- expand cases
- improve Gallery structure
- add missing project evidence
- add future pages
- improve motion
- improve documentation
- review accessibility in more detail
- strengthen copy where evidence becomes available

Post-launch work must not block Phase 1 unless it affects credibility, usability, legal completeness, or claim accuracy.

---

## Blockers

Phase 1 is not sendable if any of the following is true:

- one or more required Phase 1 pages are missing
- Floom or CUPRA does not exist as a dedicated page
- Contact is missing or unusable
- Impressum or Datenschutz is missing
- navigation is broken
- major responsive issues prevent normal review
- the site contains invented clients, KPIs, outcomes, responsibilities, research findings, testing results, testimonials, tools, or permissions
- the site looks obviously unfinished or placeholder-based
- the portfolio cannot support an application context without explanation
- a known critical design-system violation remains

---

## Decision rule

When a task is debated, ask:

"Does this help make Phase 1 sendable for applications?"

If yes, it can be prioritized.

If no, it belongs after launch.
