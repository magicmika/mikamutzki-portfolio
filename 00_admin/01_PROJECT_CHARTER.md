## Project name

MIKAMUTZKI — FINAL PORTFOLIO

---

## Project purpose

This project rebuilds mikamutzki.com as a complete senior-level portfolio system for Mika Kamutzki, Art Director, Creative Director, AI Content Engineer, and Senior UX/UI Designer in Berlin.

The portfolio must make Mika's work visible, credible, and sendable for professional applications. It should not rely only on certificates, employment references, or written credentials. The portfolio must show real work, selected visual evidence, case-study thinking, and a clear professional position.

---

## Primary goal

The primary goal is to create a sendable, senior-level portfolio that enables applications for roles in:

- Art Direction
- UX/UI Design
- Creative Direction
- Design Lead / Senior Design roles
- AI-assisted creative and digital design workflows

The portfolio should communicate Mika's combination of:

- UX/UI craft and product thinking
- Art Direction and visual judgment
- digital design and visual communication experience
- AI-assisted content and workflow maturity
- documentation discipline
- claim control
- accessibility awareness

## Phase 1 scope

Phase 1 is the first application-ready, sendable release with real work online.

Phase 1 must include:

- Home
- Work / Gallery
- Floom case
- CUPRA case
- Contact
- Impressum
- Datenschutz

These pages together must present Mika's positioning, selected work, two key case studies, curated visual proof, a clear contact route, and functional legal pages.

---

## Phase 1 purpose

Phase 1 exists to make Mika employable and presentable online as quickly as possible without falling below senior creative standards.

It does not need to be the final perfect portfolio.

It must be strong enough to send with applications.

It must show visible work, not only claims.

It must make a senior reviewer understand within seconds that Mika combines UX/UI, Art Direction, visual judgment, and AI-assisted workflow maturity.

---

## Target audience

Primary readers for Phase 1:

- Creative Directors
- Design Leads
- Heads of Design
- senior UX/UI hiring managers
- agency stakeholders
- mature in-house design teams

Secondary readers may include recruiters, HR stakeholders, and AI/transformation-oriented hiring teams.

The first impression must work for senior design decision-makers, not only for general recruiters.

---

## Desired first impression

A senior reviewer should quickly understand:

- Mika can structure complex design work.
- Mika has visual control and Art Direction judgment.
- Mika can design digital interfaces and systems.
- Mika can work with AI-assisted creative workflows responsibly.
- Mika can document and present work without inflated claims.
- Mika is credible for senior creative and digital design roles.

The site should feel sharp, focused, editorial, and controlled. It should not feel generic, decorative, unfinished, or template-based.

---

## Phase 1 proof strategy

Phase 1 needs three visible proof layers:

### 1. Floom

Floom is the UX/UI proof.

It should show product thinking, support-flow logic, interface design, process evidence, and selected screens.

### 2. CUPRA

CUPRA is the Art Direction and AI-assisted visual production proof.

It must use only confirmed claims. No campaign results, responsibilities, or impact may be invented.

### 3. Work / Gallery

Work / Gallery is the curated visual proof layer.

It should show visual range and selected work without forcing every item into a full case-study narrative.

---

## Out of scope for Phase 1

To keep Phase 1 focused and sendable, the following are out of scope for the first release:

- About page
- AI Practice page
- Leadership page
- Credentials page
- Lab / experimental section
- full design-system documentation
- complex motion and microinteractions
- complete case library with all projects deeply documented
- full long-form biography
- exhaustive process documentation for every work item

These items may be planned and added after Phase 1 is online.

---

## Quality vs. speed

Phase 1 follows a controlled middle path.

The portfolio should be online in a short time, sendable, and not embarrassing. It does not need to be perfectly polished in every detail at launch.

After launch, the site will be improved and expanded in structured iterations.

Phase 1 decisions should balance craft and speed:

- strong enough for senior review
- fast enough to support urgent applications
- focused enough to avoid overengineering
- flexible enough to improve after publishing

---

## Decision rule when stuck

When the project gets complicated or too many ideas compete, one question decides:

> Hilft das, in wenigen Tagen ein verschickbares Portfolio mit echten Arbeiten online zu bringen?

If the answer is yes, the task can be prioritized for Phase 1.

If the answer is no, the task moves to a later phase unless Mika explicitly confirms it as critical.

This rule prevents overengineering and keeps the focus on publishing a sendable senior portfolio with real work as early as possible.

---

## Claim control

Do not invent:

- clients
- KPIs
- outcomes
- responsibilities
- research findings
- testing results
- testimonials
- awards
- campaign impact
- tools
- permissions

If evidence is missing, mark it as missing or ask Mika one focused question.

Verified documents, employment references, certificates, and Mika-confirmed information may be used as evidence. Design decisions must still follow the current design-system sources.

---

## Source discipline

This project is being rebuilt as a clean project.

Old chats, old uploads, old screenshots, old prompts, historical file versions, and unconfirmed notes must not be treated as current truth.

If a statement is not confirmed, mark it as unconfirmed.

If a source conflict appears, flag it before using the information.

Do not carry old design-system mistakes into this project.

---

## Relationship to design system

This charter does not define the full design system.

Exact design-system values, tokens, typography, colors, spacing, states, components, and QA criteria must come from the current Figma Foundation and current clean design-system files.

The charter only defines project direction, scope, audience, and decision discipline.

---

## Relationship to workflow and tools

This charter does not finalize the tool workflow.

Tools and workflows will be defined step by step after they are reviewed and confirmed.

No tool should be treated as final production truth unless Mika confirms it for the current phase.
