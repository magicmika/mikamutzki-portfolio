\# 2\. CORE CREATIVE DIRECTION

Research this precise relationship between three design traditions:

\#\# Swiss Design provides order and structure

Its role is to inform:

\- grid logic  
\- hierarchy  
\- information architecture  
\- legibility  
\- typography  
\- alignment  
\- rhythm  
\- systematic consistency  
\- disciplined reduction

\#\# Bauhaus provides boldness and layout composition

Its role is to inform:

\- plakative visual impact  
\- strong shapes and fields  
\- scale  
\- asymmetry  
\- spatial composition  
\- colour relationships  
\- image and typography interaction  
\- dynamic page construction  
\- functional visual experimentation

Do not reduce Bauhaus to circles, triangles, primary-colour decoration or nostalgic pastiche.

\#\# Neobrutalism provides the statement

Its role is to inform:

\- directness  
\- confrontation  
\- visual tension  
\- deliberate rule-breaking  
\- strong interaction moments  
\- unexpected scale or placement  
\- visible construction  
\- memorable character

Neobrutalism must be used selectively as a statement, not as the dominant UI kit.

Avoid predictable neobrutalist clichés such as:

\- thick black outlines around everything  
\- comic-book drop shadows  
\- excessive boxes  
\- random primary colours  
\- playful sticker aesthetics  
\- endless pills  
\- deliberately crude usability  
\- trend-driven portfolio templates

The intended hierarchy is:

1\. Swiss Design creates the system.  
2\. Bauhaus shapes the visual stage.  
3\. Neobrutalism introduces controlled disruption and statement.

Evaluate whether this hierarchy is strategically and visually coherent. Identify productive tensions, contradictions and possible failure modes.

\- saturated

Do not recommend:

Accessibility must remain non-negotiable.

\# 5\. RESEARCH QUESTIONS

Answer the following questions with evidence and critical judgment.

\#\# Historical and conceptual foundations

1\. Which principles from International Typographic Style remain useful for contemporary digital interfaces?  
2\. Which principles become sterile, corporate or overly neutral when applied without interpretation?  
3\. Which Bauhaus principles can meaningfully influence responsive digital composition?  
4\. How can Bauhaus influence layout without producing retro imitation?  
5\. What distinguishes productive digital brutalism or neobrutalism from superficial trend styling?  
6\. How can controlled rule-breaking coexist with accessibility and usability?  
7\. How can the three traditions form one coherent contemporary system rather than a stylistic collage?

\#\# Contemporary visual culture

8\. Which current studios, designers, cultural institutions, editorial platforms, portfolios, digital products and design systems demonstrate relevant aspects of this direction?  
9\. Which references combine structure with visual impact particularly well?  
10\. Which examples avoid generic SaaS patterns?  
11\. Which examples successfully place heterogeneous creative work inside a coherent system?  
12\. Which examples use colour, typography, scale or motion as structural devices rather than decoration?  
13\. What can be learned from cultural, publishing, fashion, music, architecture and arts websites without turning the portfolio into a fashion or art template?

\#\# Design-system translation

14\. How can this direction be translated into practical foundations?  
15\. Which principles should eventually influence colour, typography, grid, spacing, shape, radius, imagery, iconography, motion and interaction?  
16\. Which decisions should remain art-direction rules rather than become tokens?  
17\. Which decisions require semantic variables?  
18\. Which expressive exceptions should not be forced into a rigid token system?  
19\. How can a strong visual identity remain scalable and maintainable?  
20\. How can accessibility be integrated without neutralising the visual statement?  
21\. How can the system support both editorial storytelling and functional UX evidence?  
22\. How should real portfolio media remain visually dominant over the system wrapper?

\#\# Portfolio validation

23\. What must the direction prove on a Home Hero?  
24\. What must it prove on a Floom Case Introduction containing colourful UX/UI evidence?  
25\. What must it prove in a Gallery Spread containing heterogeneous visual work?  
26\. Which principles must work across all three before they can become Foundation rules?  
27\. Which visual choices might work in a hero but fail in long-form case-study reading?  
28\. Which visual choices might work on desktop but collapse on mobile?  
29\. How can responsive behaviour become part of the art direction instead of a reduced desktop version?

\#\# Documentation and case-study value

30\. How should the design process be documented so the resulting system becomes a credible senior portfolio case study?  
31\. Which artifacts demonstrate actual systems thinking?  
32\. How should explorations, rejected directions, decisions, accessibility checks and implementation tests be documented?  
33\. How can AI-assisted research and exploration be shown as a mature workflow without suggesting that AI made the design decisions?  
34\. What evidence would make this case convincing to Creative Directors and Heads of Design?  
35\. What would weaken the case or make it appear fictional, inflated or process-heavy?

\#\# zeroheight

36\. What should be documented in zeroheight during exploration, after approval and after implementation?  
37\. Which information must not be documented as final before visual validation?  
38\. What is a suitable zeroheight information architecture for this portfolio system?  
39\. How should status labels such as Experimental, Candidate, Approved, Deprecated and Archive be handled?  
40\. How can zeroheight show principles, decisions, usage and rationale rather than becoming a token catalogue?  
41\. How should Figma, implementation and documentation remain aligned without creating unnecessary governance overhead?
