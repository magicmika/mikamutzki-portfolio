# 12_FOUNDATION_V2_PRINCIPLES

## Status

Living document. Principles are separated into `APPROVED`, `CANDIDATE` and `OPEN`.

## Creative thesis — APPROVED

> **Structure carries. Composition amplifies. Disruption marks.**

- Swiss Design creates the system.
- Bauhaus shapes the visual stage.
- Neobrutalism introduces controlled disruption and statement.

## Strategic tension — APPROVED

Foundation V2 must connect objective order with visible authorship. It fails when it becomes either sterile and anonymous or expressive at the cost of evidence and usability.

## Perception hierarchy — APPROVED

1. Visual authorship and compositional energy.
2. Colour, layout and total experience.
3. Work, content and narrative.
4. Precision and system competence.

Accessibility and responsive robustness are non-negotiable quality conditions across every level.

## Contextual dominance — APPROVED

> **The system leads the experience. The work leads the evidence.**

- Home and transitions: the system may dominate.
- Gallery and case introductions: system and work compose together.
- Evidence and long form: work, clarity and proof dominate.
- Statements and turning points: controlled disruption may return.

## Territory model — CANDIDATE

### Lead: Constructive Poster

Visible authorship through type, colour, media and large compositional fields. A disciplined grid operates underneath.

### Structural corrective: Signal Grid

Provides hierarchy, metadata logic, accessibility, responsive structure and consistent interaction.

### Editorial corrective: Hard Cut Editorial

Provides pacing, quiet passages, long-form readability, captions, evidence and narrative transitions.

## Candidate foundation principles

- Clear fields are stronger than excessive neutral ladders.
- Accent colour requires a defined job.
- Switzer should first deliver expression through weight, scale, spacing and line break.
- Grid should enable varied composition rather than identical repetition.
- Layout rhythm and component spacing should not be forced into one behaviour.
- Shape requires a functional reason.
- Real work is evidence; global overlays are prohibited.
- Productive motion and statement motion must be separated.
- Focus can become a deliberate visible signal.
- Mobile is a re-composition, not a stacked desktop.

## Anti-principles — APPROVED

- No decorative Bauhaus pastiche.
- No trend-neobrutalism as a UI kit.
- No muddy grey, taupe, greige or weak pastel system.
- No startup gradients or grey media overlays.
- No generic black portfolio with oversized type but no structural logic.
- No identical cards forced onto heterogeneous work.
- No large typography without semantic or compositional purpose.
- No image treatment that destroys evidence.
- No AI-polished visual language without a traceable design decision.

## Responsive composition — CANDIDATE

> **One system, multiple spatial expressions.**

- Responsive behaviour is re-composition, not proportional scaling.
- Compact prioritises sequence and vertical dramaturgy.
- Medium introduces controlled simultaneity and asymmetry.
- Standard supports the full plakative composition but is not the universal master.
- Wide uses additional horizontal space to create new relationships.
- Ultrawide treats the full viewport as an active stage instead of centring a narrow site.
- Composition surfaces and media may use the viewport; long-form reading widths remain bounded.
- Elements may behave as `Fluid`, `Bounded`, `Readable` or `Repositioned`.
- Exact breakpoints, columns, margins and scaling functions remain open until validation.

## Platform neutrality — APPROVED

- Foundation V2 is platform-independent.
- Figma is the current visual test and design source.
- Build tool, website platform and production workflow remain open.
- Lovable may later be evaluated as a fast MVP option, but is not a Foundation input.
- zeroheight remains a temporary documentation prototype, not implementation infrastructure.

## Open decisions

- Final visual validation of the territory model.
- Colour behaviour and accessible pairs.
- Light/dark surface relationship.
- Type scale and responsive typography.
- Grid, breakpoints and compositional patterns.
- Spacing rhythm and density.
- Shape, radius and divider behaviour.
- Media treatment by evidence type.
- Motion and interaction principles.
- Token and component architecture.
