## Source discipline

Use only sources classified as current for active project truth.

- `current`: may decide within its documented scope
- `draft`: may inform work but cannot finalize it
- `evidence`: may support claims within its verified boundaries
- `method`: may guide process but cannot decide content or design
- `archive` or `historical`: cannot decide current work

If current sources conflict, stop and report the conflict.

If evidence is missing, mark it as missing or ask one focused question.

Never reconstruct facts from memory, old chats or polished AI output.

## Current Foundation V2 status

Foundation V2 is under controlled exploration.

Confirmed direction:

- The portfolio should reveal creative authorship and visual energy first.
- Precision and system competence should become visible through use.
- The portfolio may have a stronger visual identity than the individual
  projects when system and project content work together coherently.
- Storytelling requires changes in visual intensity.
- Responsive behaviour means recomposition, not desktop scaling.
- Compact, medium, standard, wide and ultrawide contexts require their own
  logical spatial expressions.
- Large and ultrawide displays should use available space intentionally.
- Accessibility and readable contrast are non-negotiable.

Current territory model:

- Constructive Poster: candidate lead route
- Signal Grid: structural backbone and corrective
- Hard Cut Editorial: storytelling, rhythm and long-form corrective
- Neobrutalism: selective dramaturgical signal, not a separate territory

This territory model is an approved art-direction decision, not yet a
finished Foundation specification.

## Working method

For each meaningful task:

1. Identify the relevant source.
2. State whether the task is exploration, validation or production.
3. Distinguish confirmed decisions, hypotheses and open questions.
4. Make the smallest coherent change.
5. Preserve unrelated existing work.
6. Verify the result in proportion to its risk.
7. Update documentation when the project state changes.
8. Ask Mika for approval when a choice would materially change direction.

Do not silently broaden the scope.

Do not redesign unrelated areas during a focused correction.

After two unsuccessful narrow correction loops, stop and identify the
failure class before continuing.

## Accessibility

Accessibility is a design constraint, not a final QA decoration.

At minimum:

- test text/background contrast
- do not rely on colour alone
- avoid fine text weights on coloured backgrounds unless validated
- preserve readable text sizes and line lengths
- support keyboard and focus visibility in implementation
- respect reduced-motion preferences when motion is introduced

Do not claim WCAG compliance without the corresponding verification.
